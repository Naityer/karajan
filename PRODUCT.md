# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Operators and developers validating KARAJAN as a local AI harness router. They work in a technical console context and need to understand classification, delegation, cost, latency, health, and audit behavior without depending on paid providers.

## Product Purpose

KARAJAN receives free-form tasks, classifies complexity and risk, recommends model tiers, delegates subtasks, stores an auditable SQLite trail, and exposes operational monitoring for local routing experiments. Success means a user can trust what was delegated, why it was delegated, what it cost, and what needs review.

## Brand Personality

Precise, technical, composed. The interface should feel like a dense local control room: compact, legible, deliberate, and operational rather than decorative.

## Anti-references

Avoid marketing dashboards, oversized SaaS cards, decorative gradients, generic analytics templates, and cluttered widget mosaics that make the harness feel less auditable.

## Design Principles

- Show operational state before decoration.
- Keep density high but hierarchy obvious.
- Make every metric traceable to routing, delegation, or audit behavior.
- Use restrained accent color for selection, risk, and execution signals.
- Prefer familiar product controls over custom novelty.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target readable contrast in dark and light themes, preserve keyboard-visible controls, avoid motion that hides state, and keep responsive layouts usable on laptop-width windows.
